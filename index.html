The Biomedical Engineering Society (BMES) at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD) is dedicated to enriching the undergraduate experience for the Department of Bioengineering and those interested in Biomedical Engineering by fostering personal growth and professional development. We encourage undergraduates to get involved with their education and start making a difference today. Whether it's the fun socials with our Mentor-Mentee committee, planning our big pro-dev events with BE Day/Lab Expo, or reaching out and teaching with Outreach, come be a part of BMES at UCSD!

Bioengineering Day

Bioengineering day is a unique forum for networking between our industrial partners and the bioengineering community at UC San Diego. The undergraduate and graduate research symposia celebrate the accomplishments of our students and provide opportunities for collaboration between industry, alumni, faculty, and students. BE Day features a keynote speaker, a variety of graduate research presentations, a poster expo, an industry panel discussion, and a networking reception.

Lab Expo

This event is designed to be similar to a career fair, except that the representatives tabling at the event range from undergraduate to graduate students and professors from labs on campus. This event provides undergraduate students a chance to not only see some of the research taking place across campus, but also get involved by joining a laboratory or learning more about on-going projects. This event is to act as a stepping stone into the world of research for students who are interested in research and academia. With this event, students can be introduced to the kinds of research each lab does, and identify areas of research they would like to learn more about.

Project Team

Project Team was created as a method for teams of undergraduate students to pursue their own research interests under the guidance of a professor. Participants in Project Team will design and execute projects with the intention of creating or improving technology for the medical industry while entering these projects into competitions such as BMEIdea or BMEStart.

BE Med

BE Med is an upcoming event for this coming school year. For this event, BMES will be attempting to bridge the gap between the research and practical sides of medicine by introducing workshops, lectures, and demos from practitioners and researchers in the field.

BE Inspired

BE Inspired invites professors and graduate students to come and present a topic from their research that they themselves feel especially passionate about. The event is kept small, maxing out at around twenty-five people, to foster a more intimate connection between the presenter and the students outside of the classroom setting. The purpose behind the event is to give bioengineering students the opportunity to learn about subjects outside of the major that, due to our heavy course load, we may not be able to take on during our undergraduate career, allowing the students the opportunity for them to choose what they get out of higher education and learning.

Socials

Social activities were designed specifically to bring mentors and mentees together in an effort to create a strong bond between them. However, all members are invited to attend. The social activities are designed to separate students from school so that they can bond over more than just schoolwork. These activities were also designed so that members are given the opportunity to learn a new skill and have fun doing so.

Outreach

Outreach undergraduate volunteers focus on promoting STEM education (Science Technology Engineering and Mathematics) through local high school visits. Students are encouraged to think about simple science based solutions to real world problems and are exposed to current technological developments. They also learn about opportunities for undergraduate research, internships, and types of coursework they would take as a bioengineering major. In addition, Outreach also participates in the San Diego Festival for Science and Engineering (SDFSE) in order to provide people of all ages--from K-12 students to senior citizens--modern applications of bioengineering in an easy to understand fashion.

Mentor-Mentee Program

The purpose of the Mentor-Mentee program is to promote a sense of community in the Bioengineering Department for both underclassmen and upperclassmen. For many new freshman or transfer students, the new UCSD college environment and the Bioengineering department can be overwhelming. The BMES Mentor-Mentee Program serves to facilitate this new adjustment by making new friends who share the same academic interests. Furthermore, mentors provide these new Bioengineering students with academic and social advice to help guide them in the right direction as they embark on their undergraduate studies.
